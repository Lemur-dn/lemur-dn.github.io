Latest News


DC9 Secondment at Water Board of Lemesos, Cyprus | 2 December 2024 – 30 May 2025
DC9 completed a secondment at the Water Board of Lemesos, from 2 December 2024 to 30 May 2025. During this period, DC9 received training on water distribution networks, gained access to real-world data, and identified practical problems, which will enable the DC to apply and validate their research in a real-world setting.

2nd LEMUR Winter School - Responsible ML Winter School | 11-13 March 2025
The second LEMUR Winter School was held in Umeå in March 2025 on the topic of Responsible AI and ML. All DCs attended lectures on Responsible AI and ML, as well as hands-on multi-disciplinary and complementary training sessions. Additionally, each DC presented at the poster session, which provided opportunities for networking and professional exchange.

4th LEMUR Workshop | 11 February 2025
The 4th LEMUR Workshop was held online on February 11th, 2025, providing an opportunity for knowledge sharing and initiating collaborations among participants. All members of the project across Europe met online with presentations given by all 10 DCs to report on their research and progress, get feedback from the PIs, exchange ideas and identify possible directions and collaborations within the LEMUR project.

DC6 Secondment at KU Leuven | 16 September – 14 December 2024
DC6 completed a secondment at KU Leuven from the 16th of September to the 14th of December 2024. During this period, DC6 worked on a collaboration focused on Reinforcement Learning for Efficient Grounding in Neurosymbolic AI Systems, contributing to advancements in integrating symbolic reasoning with learning-based approaches.

Publication | NESY 2024 | 9-12 September 2024
DC3 delivered an oral presentation of their work Valid Text-to-SQL Generation with Unification-Based DeepStochLog at the 18th International Conference on Neural-Symbolic Learning and Reasoning, which took place in Barcelona, Spain in September 2024.

Publication | AIMLAI 2024 | 9 September 2024
DC4 delivered a Poster presentation of their work Target Visualization of the Backbone of Encoder LLMs at the 7th Advances in Interpretable Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence Workshop at ECML, which took place in Vilnius, Lithuania in September 2024.

Publication | IIR 2024 | 5-6 September 2024
DC8 delivered a presentation of their work Incorporating Cognitive Complexity of Text in Dense Retrieval for Personalized Search at the 14th Italian Information Retrieval Workshop, which took place in Udine, Italy, in September 2024.

3nd LEMUR Workshop | 9-10 July 2024
The third LEMUR Workshop was held in Amsterdam in July 2024, providing an opportunity for knowledge sharing and collaborations among participants.
The DCs presented their current research to the whole network and then discussed potential collaborations and possible research directions within the LEMUR project. The participants explored Amsterdam by boat in the afternoon and enjoyed dinner together.
On the second day, Kathrin Dentler (Triply) talked about her work with Knowledge Graphs and Maarten Stol (BrainCreators) presented recent work on neuro-symbolic AI. Finally, the DCs explored together the multimodal CrisisNLP dataset as part of a hackathon.

1st LEMUR Winter School - AI Winter School | 27-29 February 2024
The first LEMUR Winter School was held in Paderborn in February 2024, attended by all DCs and members of the project across Europe. The AI Winter School's purpose was to deliver insightful tutorials on important topics of AI, as well as encourage networking and future collaborations among participants.

2nd LEMUR Workshop | 1-2 February 2024
The second LEMUR Workshop was held online in February 2024, providing an opportunity for knowledge sharing and initiating collaborations among participants. All DCs and members of the project across Europe met online with the aim of introducing their work and presenting their progress to the rest of the group, and at the same time exchange ideas and possible directions within the LEMUR project.
On the second day of the Workshop, an Open Discussion Session was held, where DCs prepared a set of questions for a panel of experts (Professors Gabriella Pasi (UNIMIB), Rafael Penaloza (UNIMIB), Giuseppe Marra (KUL) and Jan-Christoph Kalo (VUA) to initiate an intriguing scientific discussion about Large Language Models (LLMs), Neuro-Symbolic AI, and Knowledge Graphs.
The final session of the Workshop was exclusively for the DCs, as final-year PhD students of another ITN project (DoSSIER) Georgios Peikos (UNIMIB) and Wojciech Kusa (TU Wien) shared their experience of participating in an ITN project.

1st LEMUR Workshop | 13-14 September 2023
The First LEMUR Workshop was held in Milan in September 2023, providing an opportunity for knowledge sharing and setting up potential collaborations among participants. All already enrolled DCs and members of the project across Europe, assembled with the aim of introducing their work to the rest of the group and at the same time exchange ideas and possible directions within the LEMUR project.
